Operational Validation Summary
Dynamic Protocol-Routing RAG System for Emergency Response Decision Support
Author: Kyle Anderson, Battalion Chief, Sacramento Fire Department
Date: April 23, 2026

Overview
This document summarizes the operational validation of the decision-support system described in the defensive publication dated April 23, 2026. Validation was conducted over approximately 8 months within Sacramento Regional Fire operations, beginning with formal training delivery in November 2025 and continuing through real incident field testing through the date of this publication.

Operational Context That Prompted Development
A series of significant HazMat incidents in 2024-2025 identified repeatable, predictable systemic gaps in emergency response decision-making. These gaps appeared consistently across incidents involving different hazard classes and different operational environments, suggesting a systemic rather than personnel-based problem.
Common systemic gaps identified across incidents included:

Delayed hazard characterization during the critical early incident window
IC task saturation during simultaneous multi-agency coordination
Inconsistent early IAP development under time pressure
Gaps in the speed and format of intel delivery from HazMat to Incident Command
FRO crews operating without adequate decision support during the HazMat arrival interval
Absence of a standardized intel workflow

The system was designed specifically to address these repeatable systemic failures without requiring changes to personnel, apparatus, or existing command structures.

Formal Training Program
A formal 3-hour training course was developed and delivered by Battalion Chief Kyle Anderson and Captain Josh Pino. The course was directed by Fire Department Operations as a direct response to operationally identified systemic gaps.
Delivery: Six sessions across two HazMat stations
Dates: November — December 2025
Audience: HazMat Specialists, HazMat Captains, and Battalion Chiefs
Estimated witnesses: 50-60 subject matter experts
The course structure included classroom instruction, systemic gap analysis, warmup scenarios, and three separate timed real-world exercises covering distinct hazard classes.

Scenario Types Validated

Underground fuel hazard rescue with atmospheric variables and confined space considerations
Large-scale toxic inhalation hazard with wind-driven plume and shifting conditions
Large-scale oxidizer release with mass casualty potential and public protective action considerations
Multi-discipline coordination scenarios involving simultaneous IC, HazMat, and FRO company operations

Performance Comparison
In controlled scenario testing conducted during formal training sessions:

An operator with no prior experience using the system produced a coherent, actionable incident action plan in approximately 1 minute
A trained HazMat team operating without the system required approximately 15 minutes to produce a less complete plan
This performance differential was observed consistently across multiple scenario types and across all six training sessions

The HazMat personnel involved are trained specialists operating within their primary discipline. The performance differential reflects system capability relative to unaided expert performance, not personnel deficiency.

Field Testing
Following formal training delivery in December 2025, the system entered real incident field testing. Testing has continued through the date of this publication across live operational incidents within Sacramento Regional Fire's jurisdiction. Field testing remains ongoing.

Current Approval Status
The system has received informal support from department leadership and technology personnel. Interest has been expressed in expanded deployment to additional public safety disciplines including law enforcement. Formal departmental approval has not yet been granted as of the date of this publication. Field testing and internal review are ongoing.

Witness Pool
Approximately 50-60 subject matter experts directly witnessed system performance during formal training sessions including HazMat Specialists, HazMat Captains, and Battalion Chiefs from Sacramento Regional Fire Department.

Intent
This validation summary is published as a companion document to the defensive publication dated April 23, 2026. Both documents are published to establish prior art and ensure the system architecture, methodology, and application described therein remain freely available to all emergency response agencies and cannot be exclusively claimed by any party.
